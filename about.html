What are User Journals?

User Journals are a method of capturing the fundamental requirements of any User Story. User Journals should be used to enhance User Stories not replace them, find out more on how to use User Journals.

Who?

Arguably the most important aspect of a User Story's narrative, who is the intended user? Use your Personas!

What?

Standard methods of defining the desired/required functionality for the users, "As a user I must..."

When?

Define at what point the user should be able to perform the action. Should the user be logged in? Have a certain permission? Before or after other actions (linked dependancies)?

Where?

Is the story part of an Epic? What part of the application does the user story impact? My account, payment page, product details etc.

Why?

Useful for defining the story priority, why does the user need this feature and why is it important? Your internal stakeholders will consider this useful.

How?

Once you have determined everything else, get sat down with your design, development and QA leads and determine what tasks are needed to fulfill the story.

How to use User Journals?

User Journals are designed to enhance User Stories with 6 familiar key questions: who, what, when, where, why and how.

Enhance

Use UJ's alongside standard User Stories to enhance them. UJ's help to make sure that the most fundamental aspects of the user's needs are capture in a succinct fashion.
